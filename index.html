In Iran, government exemplary schools are prestigious educational institutions that have been awarded a "model" license by the Ministry of Education, signifying their commitment to providing superior education. These schools are public institutions that admit students through highly competitive entrance exams. The teachers at these schools are dedicated to academic excellence, creating an environment of healthy competition among both faculty and students. Students at exemplary schools benefit from extended class hours, contributing to a rigorous academic environment. While tuition fees remain reasonable, the quality of education provided is exceptional. Admission to these schools is determined through entrance exams, typically held at the end of each academic year, with students entering from the ninth to tenth grade based on their exam performance. Those who do not pass the entrance exam may be placed on a waiting list for potential admission.
